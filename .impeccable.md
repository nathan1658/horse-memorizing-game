## Design Context

### Users
Traditional Chinese-speaking Hong Kong racing fans and casual learners who want to recognize active local horses by portrait, Chinese name, brand code, and current rating. The primary task is quick, repeatable memory practice on desktop or mobile without involving betting.

### Brand Personality
Knowledgeable, spirited, and unmistakably Hong Kong. The experience should feel like a modern race programme crossed with collectible stable cards: credible enough for a seasoned fan, playful enough for a first-time learner.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial race-day print design with turf green, racing red, warm programme paper, saddlecloth geometry, strong Traditional Chinese display type, and restrained track-line details. Light setup screens transition into a dark-green in-race environment. Avoid casino imagery, betting language, generic gradient dashboards, glassmorphism, and visual impersonation of an official HKJC product.

### Design Principles
1. Make recognition—not decoration—the star: every large photo prompt, four-name answer set, silk, code, rating, and spoken name should reinforce learning.
2. Use authentic racing-programme structure and hierarchy while clearly labeling the experience as unofficial.
3. Keep each round one tap away, with no account, tutorial gate, or wagering concepts.
4. Adapt the card grid and controls for touch, keyboard, reduced motion, and narrow Hong Kong mobile screens.
5. Celebrate mastery with race-day energy, not casino-style reward effects.
